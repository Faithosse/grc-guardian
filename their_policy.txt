# Data Protection and IT Security Policy
## Hope for Children Charity

### 1. Introduction
Hope for Children is a registered charity (number 123456) providing educational support to disadvantaged children. This policy outlines how we handle personal data and protect our IT systems.

### 2. Data We Collect
- Names and addresses of donors (approximately 2,000 records)
- Email addresses for newsletter subscribers (1,500)
- Phone numbers for event volunteers (200)
- Bank details for direct debit donors (800)
- Children's names and school information for programme participants (500)

### 3. How We Use Data
- Send quarterly newsletters and fundraising appeals
- Process donations and Gift Aid claims
- Coordinate volunteer activities
- Report to funders and the Charity Commission

### 4. Data Storage
- All records stored on Google Workspace (cloud)
- Staff access via password-protected laptops
- No encryption on local devices
- Backups performed weekly by the Office Manager

### 5. Data Retention
- Donor records: kept indefinitely for Gift Aid
- Volunteer records: kept for 2 years after last activity
- Funder reports: kept for 7 years

### 6. Security Measures
- Staff use passwords (minimum 8 characters)
- Two staff members have admin access
- No formal password policy
- No multi-factor authentication
- Laptops are not encrypted

### 7. Data Sharing
- We share donor data with our mailing house for newsletter distribution
- We share financial data with our accountant
- We do not sell data to third parties

### 8. Individual Rights
- People can email us to unsubscribe from newsletters
- We respond to data requests within 30 days
- No formal subject access request procedure

### 9. Breach Response
- The Office Manager handles any issues
- No formal breach notification procedure
- No record of past breaches
